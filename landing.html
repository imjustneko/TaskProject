Rooms

Reading. Running. Writing.
Same thing — together.

A room is a small shared checklist with a chat. Three runs a week. Three chapters by Friday. A page in your morning journal. Each member ticks their own boxes; you see the room come alive as the day goes.

  • Per-member progress. See who's caught up, who's behind, no public shaming.
  • Built-in chat. Cheer each other on, no need to message separately.
  • Invite-only. Rooms aren't public feeds. Just you and the people who said yes.

Daily memory

The week, looking back.

Snap a photo when you finish a task — your morning coffee, today's sketch, the trail at golden hour. Taskyy quietly threads them into a small visual diary you can look back on (and share, only if you want to).

  • Private by default. Memories stay yours unless you turn sharing on.
  • Weekly recap. Sunday email of what you got done — gentle, never a guilt trip.

FAQ

A few questions, answered.

Is Taskyy really free?
Yes. Daily tasks, rooms, friends, chat, and dark mode are free forever. A paid plan is coming for power-user extras like room templates, longer history, and richer recaps — never for anything that locks you in.
Do I need to add my friends for it to work?
No. Taskyy is a complete to-do app on its own. The social features quietly turn on when you add even one friend.
Can other people see what I'm doing?
Only the things you explicitly share — your status, tasks you mark as "shared", and rooms you've joined. The default is private.
Is there a mobile app?
A responsive web app today; native iOS and Android in the next couple of months.
How is this different from Notion / Todoist / Discord?
Notion is a workspace. Todoist is a solo to-do list. Discord is a hangout. Taskyy is the smallest possible thing that gets you and a friend or two doing daily small things together — and nothing else.
